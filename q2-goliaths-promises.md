
# Promises of Goliath

This research explores what the influential leaders of powerful countries - the "Goliaths" - have promised about climate goals. It also looks at how their promises could affect global warming. The aim is to understand if their actions can really help the planet.

## China

China’s President **Xi Jinping** has repeatedly vowed to peak CO₂ emissions before 2030 and achieve carbon neutrality by 2060. At the UN in September 2021 he stated, “China will strive to peak carbon dioxide emissions before 2030 and achieve carbon neutrality before 2060” [[1](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/China%E2%80%99s%20Achievements%2C%20New%20Goals%20and%20New%20Measures%20for%20Nationally%20Determined%20Contributions.pdf#:~:text=of%20the%20United%20Nations%20General,fired%20power%20projects%20abroad)]. Domestically, Beijing plans to strictly control coal use – limiting any increase over the 14th Five-Year Plan (2021–2025), and phasing down coal in the 15th [Plan](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/China%E2%80%99s%20Achievements%2C%20New%20Goals%20and%20New%20Measures%20for%20Nationally%20Determined%20Contributions.pdf#:~:text=will%20strictly%20control%20coal,China%E2%80%99s%20national%20carbon) (2026–2030) - while rapidly expanding clean energy and launching a national carbon trading market. Xi has also pledged that China will “not build new coal-fired power projects abroad,” and will support other developing countries’ clean energy transitions [[2](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/China%E2%80%99s%20Achievements%2C%20New%20Goals%20and%20New%20Measures%20for%20Nationally%20Determined%20Contributions.pdf#:~:text=of%20the%20United%20Nations%20General,fired%20power%20projects%20abroad)].

## United States

President **Joe Biden** has committed the U.S. to one of the world’s most ambitious near-term targets. At COP26 in Glasgow (Nov 2021) he announced the U.S. goal of cutting emissions 50–52% below 2005 levels by 2030 [[3](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefing-room/speeches-remarks/2021/11/01/remarks-by-president-biden-at-the-cop26-leaders-statement/#:~:text=And%20because%20we%20are%20taking,below%202005%20levels%20by%202030)] [[4](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefing-room/statements-releases/2023/04/20/fact-sheet-president-biden-to-catalyze-global-climate-action-through-the-major-economies-forum-on-energy-and-climate/#:~:text=At%20today%E2%80%99s%20meeting%2C%20the%20President,global%20challenges%2C%20like%20climate%20change)]. To achieve this, Biden’s administration is investing heavily in clean energy and efficiency – for example, offering tax credits for solar panels, weatherizing buildings, electrifying vehicles and school buses, and ramping up wind and solar manufacturing [[5](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefing-room/speeches-remarks/2021/11/01/remarks-by-president-biden-at-the-cop26-leaders-statement/#:~:text=We%E2%80%99re%20going%20to%20cut%20U,vehicles%2C%20and%20addressing%20legacy%20pollution)] [[6](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefing-room/statements-releases/2023/04/20/fact-sheet-president-biden-to-catalyze-global-climate-action-through-the-major-economies-forum-on-energy-and-climate/#:~:text=,goals%2C%20and%20decarbonizing%20international%20shipping)]. He has emphasized accelerating renewable power deployment (wind, solar and green hydrogen) and setting aggressive zero-emission vehicle targets, alongside supporting carbon capture and energy innovation through policies like the Inflation Reduction Act [[7](https://bidenwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefing-room/speeches-remarks/2021/11/01/remarks-by-president-biden-at-the-cop26-leaders-statement/#:~:text=We%E2%80%99re%20going%20to%20cut%20U,vehicles%2C%20and%20addressing%20legacy%20pollution)].


## Russia

President **Vladimir Putin** announced at an energy conference in October 2021 that Russia will “strive to be carbon neutral no later than 2060” [[8](https://www.reuters.com/business/environment/russia-striving-be-carbon-neutral-no-later-than-2060-says-putin-2021-10-13/#:~:text=MOSCOW%2C%20Oct%2013%20%28Reuters%29%20,conference%20in%20Moscow%20on%20Wednesday)]. However, Moscow’s plan relies heavily on natural sinks: Russia’s government plans to double forest carbon uptake by 2050 so that other emissions only need to fall by about half [[9](https://climateactiontracker.org/countries/russian-federation/#:~:text=In%20September%202022%2C%20the%20Russian,Siberian%20forests%20in%20recent%20years)] [[10](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/RUSSIAN_FEDERATION_cop26cmp16cma3_HLS_EN.pdf#:~:text=As%20you%20might%20be%20well,tundra%2C%20farmland%2C%20marshes%20and%20seas)]. Putin mentioned that hydrogen, ammonia and natural gas would play a larger role in the energy mix going forward [[11](https://www.reuters.com/business/environment/russia-striving-be-carbon-neutral-no-later-than-2060-says-putin-2021-10-13/#:~:text=Putin%20expects%20hydrogen%2C%20ammonia%20and,ways%20to%20tackle%20climate%20change)], and a draft 2060 climate strategy (released in 2022) relies on carbon capture/storage and land-use changes. (Russia has not announced any stronger 2030 emissions target; its last NDC only offered very modest cuts relative to a high “business-as-usual” baseline [[12](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2021/11/9/irans-failure-to-tackle-climate-change-a-question-of-priority#:~:text=According%20to%20its%20INDCs%2C%20Iran,Image)].)


## Japan

Japan’s Prime Minister **Fumio Kishida** has reaffirmed Japan’s 2050 carbon-neutrality commitment and set more ambitious 2030 goals. At COP26 (Nov 2021) he declared “being carbon neutral by 2050” and pledged to “decrease CO₂ emissions 46% by 2030 (from 2013), and pledge to strive for 50%” [[13](https://japan-forward.com/kishida-at-cop26-pledges-to-help-asia-shift-to-renewable-energy/#:~:text=%E2%80%9CBeing%20carbon%20neutral%20by%202050,the%20morning%20of%20November%202)]. At COP28 (Dec 2023) he again set a 2030 range of 46–50% reduction [[14](https://www.japan.go.jp/kizuna/2024/01/together_for_action_japan_initiatives.html#:~:text=Achieving%20the%20global%20goal%20of,by%202025%20are%20all%20required)]. Japan’s plan centers on scaling up renewables (solar, wind and hydrogen) and nuclear power, introducing carbon pricing (the new “GX Promotion” strategy and transition bonds), and phasing out coal. Kishida said Japan will make renewable energy its “main source of power” and triple global renewable capacity, and he has announced the end of new domestic coal plant construction [[15](https://www.japan.go.jp/kizuna/2024/01/together_for_action_japan_initiatives.html#:~:text=Japan%20will%20implement%20thorough%20energy,rate%20of%20energy%20efficiency%20improvements)] [[16](https://www.japan.go.jp/kizuna/2024/01/together_for_action_japan_initiatives.html#:~:text=On%20unabated%20coal%20power%20plants%2C,securing%20a%20stable%20energy%20supply)].


## Germany

Chancellor **Olaf Scholz** has reiterated Germany’s legally binding goal of climate neutrality by 2045 [[17](https://www.weforum.org/press/2023/01/german-chancellor-scholz-lays-out-roadmap-to-climate-neutrality-by-2045/#:~:text=Davos,greenhouse%20gas%20emissions%2C%20by%202045)]. He highlighted that, following the Ukraine war, Germany has accelerated its energy shift: new laws mandate rapid expansion of wind, solar and hydrogen infrastructure [[18](https://www.weforum.org/press/2023/01/german-chancellor-scholz-lays-out-roadmap-to-climate-neutrality-by-2045/#:~:text=With%20new%20laws%20mandating%20the,obstacles%20have%20been%20swept%20aside)]. Scholz announced that by 2030 roughly 80% of Germany’s electricity will come from renewable sources (roughly doubling current levels) [[19](https://www.weforum.org/press/2023/01/german-chancellor-scholz-lays-out-roadmap-to-climate-neutrality-by-2045/#:~:text=The%20German%20chancellor%20said%20for,of%20the%20decade%2C%20and%20we)]. His administration is cutting red tape for grid connections and pressing ahead with offshore wind and solar auctions to meet those targets.


## India

Prime Minister **Narendra Modi** used COP26 to declare a long-term net-zero target: India will reach carbon neutrality by 2070 [[20](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleaseIframePage.aspx?PRID=1961797#:~:text=Singh%2C%20has%20said%20India%20is,Prime%20Minister%20Shri%20Narendra%20Modi)]. He also outlined a “Panchamrit” (five nectar) plan with major 2030 goals: reach 500 GW of non-fossil (mostly solar and wind) power capacity, generate 50% of energy from renewables, cut CO₂ emissions by 1 billion tonnes (relative to 2005), and reduce carbon intensity by about 45% by 2030 [[21](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleaseIframePage.aspx?PRID=1961797#:~:text=Dr%20Jitendra%20Singh%20said%2C%20India,Zero%20emission%20target%20by%202070)]. To achieve this, India is aggressively expanding solar parks, wind farms and hydropower, launching a National Hydrogen Mission (to develop green hydrogen), and promoting efficiency and biofuels [[22](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleaseIframePage.aspx?PRID=1961797#:~:text=He%20added%20that%20apart%20from,cost%20competitive%20green%20hydrogen%20production)]. Modi has highlighted that India has already achieved ~40% power from renewables and plans to quintuple its renewables in the coming decade [[23](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleaseIframePage.aspx?PRID=1961797#:~:text=He%20added%20that%20apart%20from,cost%20competitive%20green%20hydrogen%20production)].


## United Kingdom

Prime Minister **Rishi Sunak** has reaffirmed the UK’s 2050 net-zero commitment and touted its progress. In his COP28 speech (Dec 2023) he noted UK emissions are already ~48% below 1990 levels [[24](https://www.gov.uk/government/speeches/pm-speech-at-cop28-climate-summit-4-january-2023#:~:text=And%C2%A0let%E2%80%99s%20be%20very%20clear%20%E2%80%93,the%C2%A0UK%20is%20leading%20the%20charge)] and said “we’re absolutely committed to our Net Zero targets.” Sunak announced fresh investments in clean energy and green finance: for example, a deal with Masdar and RWE to invest up to £11 billion in the UK’s Dogger Bank offshore wind project [[25](https://www.gov.uk/government/speeches/pm-speech-at-cop28-climate-summit-4-january-2023#:~:text=I%E2%80%99m%20pleased%20to%20announce%20a,deal%20between%20Masdar%20and%20RWE%E2%80%A6)], and an additional £1.6 billion for renewables, green tech and forest protection [[26](https://www.gov.uk/government/speeches/pm-speech-at-cop28-climate-summit-4-january-2023#:~:text=My%20pledge%20from%20September%20of%C2%A0%C2%A31,biggest%20single%20climate%20change%20commitment)]. He emphasized leveraging London’s financial sector to fund climate projects, while also supporting household energy efficiency and nature-based solutions alongside the technology push.


## Saudi Arabia

Crown Prince **Mohammed bin Salman** announced in Oct 2021 (at the Saudi Green Initiative forum) that Saudi Arabia will target net-zero emissions by 2060 [[27](https://www.sgi.gov.sa/knowledge-hub/hrh-the-crown-prince-and-prime-minister-announces-the-kingdom-of-saudi-arabia-s-aims-to-achieve-net-zero-emissions-by-2060/#:~:text=HRH%20the%20Crown%20Prince%20announced,economic%20prosperity%20to%20the%20Kingdom)]. Riyadh calls its approach a “carbon circular economy,” but details remain limited. The SGI framework includes near-term steps to cut emissions (roughly 278 MtCO₂ per year by 2030 through energy projects and efficiency) [[28](https://www.sgi.gov.sa/knowledge-hub/hrh-the-crown-prince-and-prime-minister-announces-the-kingdom-of-saudi-arabia-s-aims-to-achieve-net-zero-emissions-by-2060/#:~:text=HRH%20the%20Crown%20Prince%20further,carbon%20emissions%20reduction%20by%202030)], and a massive afforestation plan – planting 450 million trees by 2030 to sequester some 200 Mt of CO₂ [[29](https://www.sgi.gov.sa/knowledge-hub/hrh-the-crown-prince-and-prime-minister-announces-the-kingdom-of-saudi-arabia-s-aims-to-achieve-net-zero-emissions-by-2060/#:~:text=HRH%20the%20Crown%20Prince%20said,to%20be%20announced%20in%20the)]. Saudi officials stress continued reliance on oil/gas but plan modest boosts in renewables (aiming for 50% renewable power by 2030) and carbon capture. (As of 2024, these goals are aspirational and not yet enshrined in law.) 


## Iran

Iran’s government has not announced any new high-level CO₂ reduction target in recent years. The last official pledge was its 2015 Paris INDC, which aimed for only a 4% reduction from business-as-usual emissions by 2030 (or 12% if international sanctions were lifted) [[30](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2021/11/9/irans-failure-to-tackle-climate-change-a-question-of-priority#:~:text=According%20to%20its%20INDCs%2C%20Iran,Image)]. Iran has neither ratified the Paris Agreement nor updated that target. In practice, Iranian leaders have focused on economic concerns and access to energy, and no senior official has since set a stronger CO₂-cutting pledge.


## France

President **Emmanuel Macron** unveiled a sweeping “ecological plan” in late 2023. France will cut its greenhouse gas emissions by 55% by 2030 relative to 1990 levels [[31](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/sep/25/emmanuel-macron-ecological-plan-end-france-fossil-fuels-by-2030#:~:text=Emmanuel%20Macron%20has%20unveiled%20a,fired%20power%20plants%20by%202027)]. Key measures include phasing out all coal power by 2027 [[32](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/sep/25/emmanuel-macron-ecological-plan-end-france-fossil-fuels-by-2030#:~:text=%E2%80%9CThe%20priority%20that%20we%20have,our%20electricity%20production%2C%E2%80%9D%20he%20said)], tripling heat-pump installations, and accelerating electric vehicle and offshore wind investments [[33](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/sep/25/emmanuel-macron-ecological-plan-end-france-fossil-fuels-by-2030#:~:text=Other%20measures%20in%20the%20plan,in%20the%20regional%20train%20network)] [[34](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2023/sep/25/emmanuel-macron-ecological-plan-end-france-fossil-fuels-by-2030#:~:text=Macron%20said%20the%20state%20would,and%20train%2030%2C000%20new%20installers)]. The plan directs €10 billion toward renewable energy projects and support for France’s heavy industries to cut pollution by 45% by 2030. It also reaffirms carbon neutrality by 2050, aligning France with EU climate goals.
